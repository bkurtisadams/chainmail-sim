Game Settings

Display

Board size

The playing area in tabletop inches (24–600″). A bombard reaches 42″ and castles or forts need more room than the standard 6×4 ft table, so enlarge the board to suit. Pieces are kept inside the new bounds; press Recenter (0) to fit the whole board in view.

Army points (p.27)

Set a points target and the Units roster totals each side against it, flagging a side that goes over. Suggested Point Values are Chainmail p.27; siege equipment and thrown weapons carry no RAW cost (counted 0).

Victory check (p.27)

Once the battle begins, the roster shows each side's surviving strength — figures and points — against its starting strength. With a break point set, a side that drops below it is flagged. The sim never declares a winner or ends the game; that call stays yours (Chainmail p.27).

Figures & scale (p.8)

Figure scale shrinks the unit markers to mirror smaller castings — 15mm is half the size of the default 30mm. Fortifications, terrain and all distances stay put, so smaller figures sit in proportion to the works; and because contact and ranges are measured edge to edge, they tighten with the figures, exactly as smaller bases do on the table. Default unit size pre-fills the Count field for a new unit (1 figure ≈ 20 men, p.8), so the default need not be a 400-man block.

Optional Rules

Charge impetus bonus, prisoners step, and cavalry-charge morale check will appear here as their slices land. Chainmail p.16–18.

Movement (p.9–11)

Each unit's distance moved this turn is tracked against its type's allowance (the drag ruler and the unit pane show it). Off (default) is advisory: you can move freely and the sim only warns when a unit exceeds its allowance, so freeform placement still works. On caps each unit's movement at the rulebook limit. Resets every round.
